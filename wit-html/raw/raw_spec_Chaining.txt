Interactive diagrams are programs.  This is the program structure for the interactive diagram which shows who is who at the AU19 Audacity conference:

<pre>
VERTICAL_LAYOUT
  RGBA_FOCUS_DETAIL Focus=SmallCircle
</pre>

The specification above builds a simple focus-detail display.  There's a
stand-alone [https://wit.audacityteam.org/demos.htm?page=Maps
interactive demo] but here is the result in wiki:

-----
{{#widget:Dynamic|page=Maps|height=620px}}
-----

<h1>Climate Zones</h1>
This is the Köppen climate classification map.  Temperature and precipitation
both play a part in determining climate zones.

<pre>SECTION=1</pre>
<pre>CLICK LOAD SPEC=</pre>
[[Toolbox/Chaining]]

<pre>NEXTOBJECT:TIP=</pre>
<h1>Vegetation</h1>
Closely allied to climate zones is the vegetation map.

<pre>SECTION=2</pre>
<pre>CLICK LOAD SPEC=</pre>
[[Toolbox/Chaining]]

<pre>NEXTOBJECT:TIP=</pre>
<h1>Ocean Currents</h1>
Shows the 'great conveyor belt', cold water flowing deep down, warmed water
flowing in the opposite direction near the surface.  Unfortunately this map
does not join up properly East-West.

<pre>SECTION=0</pre>
<pre>CLICK LOAD SPEC=</pre>
[[Toolbox/Chaining]]

<pre>NEXTOBJECT:TIP=</pre>
<h1>Plate Techtonics</h1>
Shows the boundaries between plates, where seismic and volcanic activity are
most likely.  Includes 'the great ring of fire'.

<pre>NEXTOBJECT:TIP=</pre>
<h1>Moon</h1>
An infra red map of the moon.  The 'dark side' of the moon is not actually
any more dark than the other, it is just that it is always turned away from us.
 The side near to us is smoother than the more pock marked side further away.
